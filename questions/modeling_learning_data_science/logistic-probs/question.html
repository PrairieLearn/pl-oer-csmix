Consider the following logistic regression model with known values of the parameters. $$ \log\left(\frac{p(\boldsymbol{x})}{1 - p(\boldsymbol{x})}\right) = {{params.beta_0}} + {{params.beta_1}} x_1 + {{params.beta_2}} x_2 $$ $$ p(\boldsymbol{x}) = P\left[ Y = 1 \mid \boldsymbol{X} = \boldsymbol{x} \right] = P\left[ Y = 1 \mid X_1 = x_1, X_2 = x_2 \right] $$ *** Use this model to do the following: - **(a)** *Calculate* $P\left[ Y = 1 \mid \boldsymbol{X} = \boldsymbol{x} \right]$ when $x_1 = {{params.x1a}}$ and $x_2 = {{params.x2a}}$. - **(b)** *Calculate* $P\left[ Y = 1 \mid \boldsymbol{X} = \boldsymbol{x} \right]$ when $x_1 = {{params.x1b}}$ and $x_2 = {{params.x2b}}$. - **(c)** *Calculate* $P\left[ Y = 0 \mid \boldsymbol{X} = \boldsymbol{x} \right]$ when $x_1 = {{params.x1c}}$ and $x_2 = {{params.x2c}}$. - **(d)** *Calculate* $P\left[ Y = 0 \mid \boldsymbol{X} = \boldsymbol{x} \right]$ when $x_1 = {{params.x1d}}$ and $x_2 = {{params.x2d}}$. ***

*** Two functions from `scipy` could be useful here: - `scipy.special.logit` is the logit function. - `scipy.special.expit` is the sigmoid function.
